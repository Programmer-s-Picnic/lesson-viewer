Step 2 — Screen calibration

Place a real bank card, ATM card, or similar card against the screen. Use the slider until the on-screen rectangle matches the real card size. Standard bank card size is about 85.6 mm × 53.98 mm.

Important warnings before using this tool

Please read carefully. This tool is for basic screening and educational use only.

Primary disclaimer
  • This tool is NOT a medical device and does NOT provide a valid eyeglass prescription.
  • It is only for basic vision screening and educational purposes.
  • Results may be inaccurate due to screen size, brightness, viewing distance, lighting conditions, and user input.
  • Always consult a qualified optometrist or ophthalmologist for a proper eye examination and prescription.
Accuracy limitations
  • This tool cannot accurately measure astigmatism cylinder power and axis.
  • It cannot assess eye disease, binocular vision, or exact prescription needs.
  • Any values shown are rough estimates only.
Usage conditions
  • Use in a well-lit room.
  • Maintain the instructed viewing distance.
  • Keep screen brightness comfortable.
  • Test one eye at a time when required.
  • Improper setup can lead to incorrect results.
Stop immediately if uncomfortable
  • Stop if you feel eye strain, headache, dizziness, blurred vision, or double vision.
  • Do not continue if you feel uncomfortable.
High-risk users should not rely on this
  • Do not rely on this tool for children.
  • Do not rely on it if you have diabetes, known eye disease, recent eye surgery, or sudden worsening of vision.
  • Seek professional medical advice instead.
Result interpretation
  • Do not use the results to order glasses online.
  • Do not use them to change an existing prescription.
  • Do not self-diagnose vision problems using this app.
Liability notice
  • By using this tool, you acknowledge that the results are approximate and that the creators are not responsible for decisions made based on these results.
